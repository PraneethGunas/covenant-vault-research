\section{Simplicity on Elements: The Unoccupied Optimal Corner}
\label{app:simplicity}

\subsection{Scope and motivation}
\label{app:simplicity:scope}

Simplicity~\cite{OC17} is an \emph{alternative script primitive}---a
typed combinator calculus with Coq-verified denotational
semantics---deployed on Elements~\cite{DPNS16}, a Bitcoin-adjacent
sidechain whose blocks are produced by known functionaries rather
than by proof-of-work miners. Execution uses native-code
\emph{jets} for known combinator subtrees. Our Simplicity vault is
excluded from the formal results of Section~\ref{sec:impossibility}
and reported here as a cross-substrate reference: it demonstrates
that the dominant corner $D^\star$ of
Section~\ref{sec:imposs:design_space} is occupiable on some
substrate, even while no Bitcoin proposal occupies it.

\subsection{Capabilities and design-space position}
\label{app:simplicity:axes}

Our Simplicity vault on Elements supports per-output binding on both
trigger and recovery paths, atomic or bounded partial withdrawal,
cold-key recovery with an optional keyless post-timeout sweep, and
MAX\_FEE-capped in-value fee deduction. In the atomic configuration
with cold-key-only recovery, it occupies a strict-best position on
every axis where such a position exists:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \axauth{} = \texttt{cold-key}: Schnorr signature check on the
    recovery leaf. An optional construction exposes a keyless sweep to
    a pre-committed heir address after a timeout.
  \item \axrevault{} = \texttt{no} (atomic) or \texttt{yes-bounded}
    (bounded-partial with a compile-time residual-floor covenant that
    structurally caps UTXO count).
  \item \axfee{} = \texttt{dynamic}: in-value deduction capped at a
    compile-time \texttt{MAX\_FEE} ceiling.
  \item \axwdbind{} = \texttt{dedicated-jets-combinator}: binds output 0
    via \texttt{jet::output\_script\_hash(0)} and
    \texttt{jet::output\_amount(0)} at compile time.
  \item \axrecbind{} = \texttt{dedicated-jets-combinator}: same jets
    bind the recovery output.
  \item \axopsurf{} = \texttt{single-mode}.
  \item \axintro{} = \texttt{typed-combinator-introspection}.
\end{itemize}

In the atomic configuration, the vault is immune by construction to
every attack class \classfee{}--\classmode{} identified in
Section~\ref{sec:impossibility}. It occupies the strict-best position
on \axauth{}, \axrevault{}, \axwdbind{}, \axrecbind{}, and \axintro{}
simultaneously; no Bitcoin proposal does. The bounded-partial
construction admits a bounded class-\classscale{} exposure governed
by the residual-floor covenant; the keyless-post-timeout construction
introduces a class-\classgrief{} / class-\classcold{} timing trade-off
after the timeout.

\subsection{Lifecycle measurement}
\label{app:simplicity:empirical}

The Simplicity vault lifecycle totals $865\vB{}$ (deposit $269$,
trigger $298$, withdraw $298$, recover $286$), $49$--$135\%$ larger
than the Bitcoin designs of Table~\ref{tab:vsizes}. This measurement
is for the atomic configuration with cold-key-only recovery. The
overhead bundles Elements' serialisation ($30$--$40$ bytes per output
for confidential-transaction fields even in non-confidential mode) and
Simplicity's bit-oriented DAG witness format. The bounded-partial and
keyless-post-timeout constructions share the same covenant
infrastructure; their lifecycle vsizes have not yet been measured.

The MAX\_FEE design caps in-value fee deduction at a compile-time
ceiling, with the signer choosing the actual fee at broadcast.

\subsection{Foundations and substrate}
\label{app:simplicity:foundations}

Simplicity's Coq-verified denotational semantics give stronger
guarantees than Alloy bounded model checking of the Bitcoin Script
extensions can produce --- a Simplicity program's meaning is
determined by its Merkle root, under a formal semantics that has
been mechanically verified. Simplicity on Liquid is live today under
\emph{federation trust}; the four Bitcoin Script extensions remain
under \emph{Bitcoin consensus review}. The substrate choice is a
security-relevant decision alongside the per-covenant analysis of
Section~\ref{sec:impossibility}: a vault on Simplicity inherits the
federation's liveness and censorship properties; a vault on Bitcoin
inherits proof-of-work's. The dominant corner being occupiable on
Simplicity is therefore not a turn-key prescription to migrate vaults
to Liquid; it is evidence that the unoccupied status among current
Bitcoin proposals reflects a consensus-substrate constraint rather
than a design impossibility.
